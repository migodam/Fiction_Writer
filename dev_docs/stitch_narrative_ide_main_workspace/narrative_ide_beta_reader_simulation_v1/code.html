Chapter 4: The Silent Council

Elara pushed open the heavy oak doors, the wood groaning in protest. The council chamber was vast, lit only by the flickering light of braziers suspended from the vaulted ceiling. Shadows danced across the faces of the seven elders seated in a semicircle before her.

She recognized the figure standing in the center of the room immediately, despite the heavy cloak. It was Malakor, the very man who had supposedly died in the siege of Oakhaven three moons ago. "I told you I would return," he rasped, pulling back his hood.

Predictable Twist
"Saw this coming from Chapter 1. The foreshadowing was too heavy." - Fantasy Fan

The elders murmured among themselves, a low susurration that sounded like dry leaves in winter. Elara stepped forward, her hand resting on the pommel of her sword. She had expected a trap, but not this. Not him.

Malakor produced a small, crystalline orb from his robes. It glowed with a sickly green light. "The Arcanum of Vael has authorized the immediate dissolution of the pact," he declared, referencing the complex geopolitical treaty established during the Third Era of the Sun-King's reign, specifically the addendum regarding magical oversight in neutral territories.

Confusion / Pacing
"Too much exposition dumped here. Lost the tension of the reveal." - Casual Reader

Elara drew her blade, the steel ringing clearly in the sudden silence. "The Arcanum has no jurisdiction here, Malakor. And you have no right to speak for them."
