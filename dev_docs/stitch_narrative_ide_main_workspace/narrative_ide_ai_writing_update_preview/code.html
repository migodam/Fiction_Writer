Chapter 4: The Hidden Chamber

Elara's breath caught as she ran her fingers over the cold stone. The faint outline of a door was barely visible in the torchlight. She glanced nervously over her shoulder; the echoing footsteps of the palace guards were growing louder.

"It has to be here," she muttered, her hands frantically searching for a mechanism. The map had been clear, but maps rarely accounted for centuries of dust and decay.

A sudden click resonated through the damp stone. Elara hadn't found a keyhole, but a loose brick slightly discolored by ancient moss. As she pressed it, a narrow compartment slid open, revealing not a key, but an intricate, star-shaped metallic object.

"A cipher," she whispered. Her heart pounded against her ribs. The footsteps were at the end of the hall now. She grabbed the cipher, noting a sudden, strange warmth radiating from its center—a magical signature she hadn't anticipated.

She slammed the compartment shut just as the heavy oak doors to the corridor burst open. "Halt!" a gruff voice commanded. She spun around, the cipher hidden quickly within the folds of her cloak, trying to compose her terrified expression into one of innocent confusion.
